# Get-OrchJobVideo

## SYNOPSIS
Gets jobs with video recordings attached from UiPath Orchestrator.

## SYNTAX

```
Get-OrchJobVideo [[-Path] <String[]>] [-Recurse] [[-Skip] <UInt64>] [[-First] <UInt64>]
 [-ProgressAction <ActionPreference>] [<CommonParameters>]
```

## DESCRIPTION
The Get-OrchJobVideo cmdlet retrieves jobs that have video recordings attached from UiPath Orchestrator. Video recordings are created when job video recording is enabled in the robot settings, allowing you to review the execution of automated processes visually. This cmdlet helps identify jobs with video content for analysis, debugging, or compliance purposes.

This is a folder entity operation. You must either navigate to a specific folder using Set-Location (cd) or use the -Path or -Recurse parameters to specify target folders. The -Path parameter specifies target folder paths. If not specified, the current folder will be targeted.

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Get jobs with video from current folder
```powershell
PS C:\> cd Orch1:\root
PS Orch1:\root> Get-OrchJobVideo
```

Navigates to the root folder and retrieves all jobs with video recordings from that folder.

### Example 2: Get jobs with video recursively
```powershell
PS C:\> Get-OrchJobVideo -Recurse
```

Retrieves all jobs with video recordings from all folders recursively, starting from the current folder.

### Example 3: Get jobs with video from specific path
```powershell
PS C:\> Get-OrchJobVideo -Path Orch1:\root\MyProject
```

Retrieves jobs with video recordings from the specified folder without changing the current location.

### Example 4: Get first 10 jobs with video
```powershell
PS C:\> Get-OrchJobVideo -Recurse -First 10
```

Retrieves the first 10 jobs with video recordings from all folders recursively.

### Example 5: Get jobs with video using skip and first
```powershell
PS C:\> Get-OrchJobVideo -Recurse -Skip 5 -First 10
```

Skips the first 5 jobs with video recordings and then gets the next 10 jobs.

### Example 6: Get jobs with video from multiple paths
```powershell
PS C:\> Get-OrchJobVideo -Path Orch1:\root\Project1, "Orch1:\root\Project2"
```

Retrieves jobs with video recordings from multiple specified folder paths.

### Example 7: Get job video details and examine structure
```powershell
PS C:\> Get-OrchJobVideo -Recurse | Select-Object -First 1 | ConvertTo-Json -Depth 5
```

Retrieves the first job with video recording and displays its complete object structure in JSON format for detailed analysis.

### Example 8: Filter jobs with video by creation date
```powershell
PS C:\> Get-OrchJobVideo -Recurse | Where-Object {$_.CreationTime -gt (Get-Date).AddDays(-7)} | Select-Object Id, ReleaseName, CreationTime
```

Gets all jobs with video recordings from the last 7 days and displays key information.

## NOTES
- This cmdlet operates on folder entities and requires folder navigation or path specification
- Video recordings are only available for jobs where video recording was enabled in the robot settings
- For optimal performance, place -Path and -Recurse parameters immediately after the cmdlet name to enable proper autocompletion
- Use -First and -Skip parameters for pagination when dealing with large numbers of jobs with video recordings
- Video recordings consume significant storage space and may have retention policies applied
- Not all job executions will have video recordings - this depends on robot configuration and process settings
- Video recordings are typically used for debugging, compliance, or training purposes
